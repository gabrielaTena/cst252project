Cosplay

Cosplay is a hobby that has been practiced for decades. It's become more mainstream as it's grown in popularity. There are many types of cosplay, but the most popular types are anime, video game and comic book cosplay. The costumes vary from simple clothing to elaborate ones with special effects and props. Some people even go as far as to alter their skin color or wear prosthetics to look like their favorite characters. Cosplayers sometimes do this for the purpose of self-expression, while others do it for the attention they receive from other people at conventions or gatherings.

Cosplay is a form of performance art in which participants wear costumes and fashion accessories to represent a specific character or idea. Cosplay can be seen as a form of creative expression, as well as an act of fandom. It can also be argued that cosplayers are often viewed by their fans as role models. Cosplayers typically interact with those who enjoy their work at conventions and other events. [E3 or Anime Expo]

2000's

Clothing from the 2000s was directly influenced by globalization, the rise of fast fashion, and celebrities as style icons.


Technology and Y2K had a huge impact on fashion in the early 2000s. Denim became a staple for men and women, going beyond jeans to shirts, jackets, and hats.


As the decade went on, 2000s fashion began to take cues from 1960s bohemian looks. Low-rise jeans, cropped jackets, and dresses over jeans were popular outfit choices for women. For men outfit options included light wash bootcut jeans, cargo shorts, and polos with popped collars.
